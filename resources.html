wondering where to begin?

yoga resources ♡

When I first started yoga, I felt both inspired and overwhelmed — there were so many styles, teachers, and videos that I didn’t know what to choose. I just wanted something that felt right for me.

If you’ve ever felt the same, I’ve gathered a few simple, beginner-friendly resources to help you begin your practice with confidence and curiosity.

sun salutations (surya namaskar)

Sun Salutations are the heart of many yoga practices — a rhythmic sequence that connects breath, movement, and mindfulness. Below are three traditional variations to explore: the classic Hatha Surya Namaskar, and the dynamic A and B sequences found in Vinyasa and Ashtanga styles.

Hatha Surya Namaskar

A step-by-step tutorial on the Hatha Sun Salutation sequence, perfect for beginner flows.

Surya Namaskar A

A foundational vinyasa flow linking breath and movement in a smooth, energizing rhythm.

Surya Namaskar B

A slightly longer sequence that introduces Chair Pose and Warrior I, building heat and strength.

full-body flows

These gentle flows build comfort, body awareness, and breath connection — perfect for easing into movement.

30-minute Stretch (No Props)

Unwind from head to toe with slow, mindful movement.

45-minute Power Yoga

An intermediate full-body yoga flow, great for building strength.

build strength and mobility

Once you feel comfortable with foundational movements, explore flows that develop strength and joint stability while keeping your practice grounded and balanced.

Full Body Strength & Flexibility

Dynamic sequences to support balance and endurance.

Gentle Flexibility Practice

Soft mobility work to support posture and daily comfort.

♡ kat's tips for new yogis

  • Start slow. Even 10 minutes or 3 rounds of Sun Salutations can go a long way.
  • Focus on the breath before the movements.
  • Use props such as cushions, books, or rolled towels.
  • Modify freely. Yoga meets you where you are.
  • Don’t worry about looking “right” — focus on how it feels.
  • Practice kindness toward your body, your breath, and your process. Everyone’s journey looks different.
